Abstract

This simulation models three tax planning scenarios using a stylized gold mining firm in a developing country. Users explore how aggressive tax strategies—transfer pricing, thin capitalization, and commodity hedging, erode host-country revenues, and how countermeasures can restore fiscal capacity.
